For its first five years 1990-1995, HTML went through a number of revisions and experienced a number of extensions. In 2003, the publication of XForms, a technology which was positioned as the next generation of Web forms. This interest was borne from the realization that XML's deployment as a Web technology was limited to entirely new technologies rather than as a replacement for existing deployed technologies like HTML.

In 2006, the W3C indicated an interest to participate in the development of HTML5 after all, and in 2007 formed a working group chartered to work with the WHATWG on the development of the HTML5 specification.

JavaScipt was create by Brendan Eich in 1995 during his time at Nescape Communications. It was inspired by Java, Scheme and Self. Netscape for a time, made the best browser in the world and enjoyed market dominance. In late 1995, when Microsoft cottoned-on to the competitive threat the Web posed, the Internet Explorer project was started in an all-out attempt to wrestle control of the emerging platform from Netscape.
