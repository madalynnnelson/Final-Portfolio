I have been going to Davenport West High School for about a year and a half now. I have made lots of friends and memories here, playing softball and joining the robotics teams. My freshmen year I was on The Combustiable Lemons and my sophomore year I'm on the Disruptive Innovation team which is FRC while last year was FTC.

We have a FRC team called Disruptive Innovation, it is like FTC but this Competition has very big robots instead of little ones. For Disruptive Innovation this year I am one of the communations/outreach lead along with a fellow student that's in the daytime class. We have been making great progress and already making a difference, for veterans day we had an aud for our veterans. We had people come and talk about what branch they were in and their own experience in the military.With Disruptive Innovation this year 2019-2020, we didn't have a lot of people left with experience. Last year 2018 we had a lot on seniors, so that means they were leaving at the end of the year.

With outreach we have been doing LEGO robotics for little kids for about four years now, with FLL Jr. this year the theme is contruction/ building and so far the kids are doing great.The kids are learning a lot and having so much fun programming the little robot they have to practice on.Team 6317 has been competing in the FIRST Robotics Competition for 4 seasons, and a total of 8 official events. They were a rookie (and began competing) in 2017 and remain active as of the last season, 2019.The students have been competing in St. Louis, Missouri at the FIRST Tech Challenge since Tuesday. The robots go through round after round of judging and elimination with the other robots.Disruptive Innovation was awarded the Chairmen’s Award at the Seven Rivers Regional in La Crosse, WI. The Award advances West’s FRC team to the World Championship in Detroit from April 24th to 27th 2018.

The Combustible Lemons is a FTC team that has went to worlds and won. The Combustible Lemons is FIRST Tech Challenge Team 5466 from Davenport West High School, Davenport, IA. It is made up of 14 students ranging from 9th to 12th grade, and 5 coaches/mentors. Each year the team builds a robot for the annual FTC competition. During the 2011-2012 season, the team was on the winning alliance at the Iowa Championship, won the Inspire Award at the Michigan Championship, and attended the World Championship. This year we are captains of the finalist alliance at the Solon Qualifier and have qualified to go to the Iowa State Championship. The Combustiable Lemons went to worlds back in 2016 and the Mortal Combots got to State.They're evaluated on things like build, speed, and accuracy.
